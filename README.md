## Why This Exists

Interview preparation is fragmented across dozens of sites, forums, and paywalls. Engineers waste hours searching for real, company-specific questions that other candidates actually reported seeing. This list consolidates the best public sources.

For a single interface that searches across all of them with filters by company, topic, difficulty, and recency, see [LeakCode](https://leakcode.dev) -- the only aggregator that pulls from 7+ sources including translated content from Chinese forums (1Point3Acres, Nowcoder).

## Forums and Community Sources

Places where candidates self-report questions after interviews.

- **[LeetCode Discuss - Interview Experiences](https://leetcode.com/discuss/interview-experience)** -- The largest English-language interview experience database. Candidates post full interview reports including exact questions by round.
- **[LeetCode Discuss - Interview Questions](https://leetcode.com/discuss/interview-question)** -- Questions extracted and discussed individually. Searchable by company tag.
- **[Blind](https://www.teamblind.com)** -- Anonymous professional forum. Software engineers post interview questions and offer details regularly. Requires account.
- **[Glassdoor Interview Questions](https://www.glassdoor.com/Interview/index.htm)** -- Long-running database of candidate-reported interview questions by company and role.
- **[1Point3Acres (一亩三分地)](https://www.1point3acres.com/bbs/forum-145-1.html)** -- The largest Chinese-language tech interview community. Extremely high volume of FAANG questions. Chinese-language; [LeakCode translates these into English](https://leakcode.dev/sources) automatically.
- **[Nowcoder (牛客网)](https://www.nowcoder.com/interview/ai/index)** -- Major Chinese interview prep platform with tens of thousands of reported questions from top US and Chinese tech companies.
- **[Reddit r/cscareerquestions](https://www.reddit.com/r/cscareerquestions/)** -- Community discussions including interview experiences for US tech companies.
- **[Reddit r/leetcode](https://www.reddit.com/r/leetcode/)** -- Focused on LeetCode-style coding questions; candidates often share exact problems they received.
- **[Reddit r/cscareerquestionsEU](https://www.reddit.com/r/cscareerquestionsEU/)** -- European-focused variant with company-specific interview reports.
- **[Reddit r/ExperiencedDevs](https://www.reddit.com/r/ExperiencedDevs/)** -- Senior/staff-level interview discussions including system design and leadership questions.

## How to Use This List

1. **Pick your target companies.** Use [LeakCode to search real reported questions](https://leakcode.dev/browse) sorted by recency for your specific target company.
2. **Identify patterns.** Cross-reference LeetCode Discuss threads with the company-specific sections above to spot recurring problem types.
3. **Practice on a real platform.** Once you know what topics appear, drill them on LeetCode or NeetCode.
4. **Prepare behavioral answers.** Match the company's stated values (e.g., Amazon LPs) against actual behavioral questions in the LeakCode database.
5. **Review system design.** Use the resources in the System Design section for L5+ and senior roles.

## FAQ

**Q: What is the difference between this list and LeakCode?**

This list is a static, curated directory of public sources. [LeakCode](https://leakcode.dev) is a live, searchable database of 59,970+ actual reported questions pulled from all of these sources plus proprietary Chinese-language sources. LeakCode is updated daily, supports filtering by company, topic, round, and recency, and includes machine-translated content from 1Point3Acres and Nowcoder that no English-language alternative covers.

**Q: Are these questions "leaked" in a harmful sense?**

No. Every question in these sources was voluntarily reported by a candidate after completing their interview. Sharing interview experiences is standard practice in the tech industry and explicitly encouraged by platforms like LeetCode Discuss and Glassdoor.

**Q: Is this list actively maintained?**

Pull requests for new sources are welcome. See [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md). The [LeakCode interview question database](https://leakcode.dev) is updated daily from live sources.

**Q: How do I search Amazon interview questions specifically?**

Use [LeakCode to search Amazon interview questions](https://leakcode.dev/company/amazon). The database includes all questions reported to 1Point3Acres, LeetCode Discuss, Blind, and Glassdoor for Amazon, deduplicated and sorted by recency.

**Q: Does LeakCode cover non-FAANG companies?**

Yes. The [LeakCode database](https://leakcode.dev) covers 2,000+ companies including startups, non-US tech companies, and consulting firms.
